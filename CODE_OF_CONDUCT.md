# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

* Project repositories
* Issue trackers
* Discussion forums
* Email communications
* Social media interactions
* In-person events related to the project
* Representing the project in public spaces

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing
the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include:
* Using an official e-mail address
* Posting via an official social media account
* Acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of
acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive,
or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are
not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation
decisions when appropriate.

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

**Primary Contact**: Open an issue with the `conduct` label  
**Private Contact**: Direct message to project maintainers  
**Urgent Issues**: Mark reports as urgent in the subject line

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### What to Include in Your Report

To help us address the issue effectively, please include:

1. **Your contact information** (if you're comfortable sharing)
2. **Names or usernames** of people involved (if applicable)
3. **Description of the incident** including:
   - What happened
   - When it happened
   - Where it happened (link to issue, PR, etc.)
   - Context surrounding the incident
4. **Any supporting evidence** (screenshots, links, etc.)
5. **Whether you've reported this before** and what the outcome was
6. **What response you'd like** from the community leaders

### Confidentiality

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the
reporter of any incident. We will:

* Keep your report confidential
* Not share your identity without your permission
* Take all reports seriously
* Investigate all reported incidents

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining
the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing
clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the
behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of
actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No
interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with
those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This
includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels
like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent
ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public
communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or
private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction
with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period.
Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community
standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an
individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the
community.

## Educational Context

### Additional Guidelines for Learning Environments

This project is educational in nature. We expect:

#### For Learners:
* **Ask questions freely** - No question is too basic
* **Share your learning journey** - Help others learn from your experiences
* **Respect different learning paces** - Everyone learns differently
* **Provide constructive feedback** - Help improve code and documentation
* **Credit sources appropriately** - Acknowledge where you learned concepts

#### For Instructors/Mentors:
* **Be patient** - Remember your own learning journey
* **Provide clear explanations** - Use examples and analogies
* **Encourage experimentation** - Safe failures lead to learning
* **Give constructive feedback** - Focus on improvement, not criticism
* **Acknowledge all levels** - From beginners to experts

#### For Code Reviewers:
* **Be kind in reviews** - Assume good intentions
* **Explain the "why"** - Don't just point out what's wrong
* **Suggest improvements** - Provide examples of better approaches
* **Acknowledge good work** - Positive reinforcement matters
* **Focus on code, not people** - Critique the code, not the coder

## Our Values

### Inclusivity
We welcome contributors from all backgrounds and experience levels. Whether
you're a student, self-taught developer, or seasoned professional, your
contributions are valued.

### Education
We prioritize learning and teaching. Making mistakes is part of the learning
process. We encourage questions and experimentation.

### Collaboration
We believe in working together to create better solutions. Different
perspectives make our code and documentation stronger.

### Respect
We treat all community members with dignity and respect, valuing their time,
contributions, and perspectives.

### Quality
We strive for excellence while recognizing that perfection is a journey, not a
destination.
